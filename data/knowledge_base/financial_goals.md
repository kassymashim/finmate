# Setting and Achieving Financial Goals

## SMART Financial Goals Framework
Every financial goal should be:
- Specific: "Save $15,000 for a house down payment" not "save more money"
- Measurable: Track progress with exact numbers
- Achievable: Based on current income and expenses
- Relevant: Aligned with your life priorities
- Time-bound: "By December 2025" not "someday"

## Short-Term Goals (< 1 year)
- Build $1,000 emergency starter fund
- Pay off a specific credit card
- Save for a vacation ($2,000-5,000)
- Build one month of expenses as buffer
- Reduce dining out spending by 30%

## Medium-Term Goals (1-5 years)
- Build full emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
- Save for house down payment (20% ideal, 10% minimum)
- Pay off all non-mortgage debt
- Save for a major purchase (car, wedding)
- Start investing consistently

## Long-Term Goals (5+ years)
- Retirement savings (aim for 25x annual expenses)
- Children's education fund
- Financial independence (passive income covers expenses)
- Generational wealth building

## Goal Prioritization Matrix
Priority 1: Financial safety (emergency fund, insurance, basic debt payments)
Priority 2: Eliminating toxic debt (high-interest credit cards, payday loans)
Priority 3: Retirement basics (enough to get employer match)
Priority 4: Accelerated debt payoff
Priority 5: Major savings goals (house, education)
Priority 6: Wealth building (additional investments, real estate)

## Tracking Progress
- Review goals monthly (15 minutes)
- Adjust targets quarterly if income or circumstances change
- Celebrate milestones (every 25% progress)
- Use visual trackers (progress bars, charts)
- Share goals with accountability partner

## Common Pitfalls
- Setting too many goals at once (focus on 2-3 maximum)
- Not adjusting for inflation (add 3% annually to long-term goals)
- Ignoring lifestyle changes (new baby, job change, relocation)
- All-or-nothing thinking (partial progress still matters)
- Comparing to others (your timeline is yours)

## Monthly Financial Review Checklist
1. Did I hit my savings target?
2. Where did I overspend? Why?
3. Are there expenses I can eliminate next month?
4. Am I on track for my quarterly/annual goals?
5. Do any goals need adjustment?
6. What's one financial win from this month?
