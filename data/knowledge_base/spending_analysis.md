# Spending Analysis Framework

## How to Analyze Spending Patterns

### Step 1: Categorize All Transactions
Group expenses into meaningful categories:
- Fixed expenses: Same amount every month (rent, subscriptions, loan payments)
- Variable necessities: Essential but fluctuating (groceries, utilities, gas)
- Discretionary: Non-essential (dining out, entertainment, shopping)
- Periodic: Irregular but predictable (car maintenance, medical, gifts)

### Step 2: Calculate Category Ratios
For each category, determine:
- Percentage of total income
- Month-over-month change
- Comparison to recommended benchmarks

### Recommended Spending Benchmarks (% of after-tax income)
- Housing: 25-35% (includes rent/mortgage, insurance, property tax)
- Transportation: 10-15% (car payment, gas, insurance, maintenance)
- Food: 10-15% (groceries + dining out, ideally 70/30 split)
- Utilities: 5-10%
- Healthcare: 5-10%
- Savings: 15-20%
- Entertainment: 5-10%
- Personal/Shopping: 5-10%

### Step 3: Identify Spending Triggers
Common overspending triggers:
- Emotional spending (stress, boredom, celebration)
- Social pressure (keeping up with peers)
- Convenience spending (too tired to cook, parking vs walking)
- Subscription creep (adding services without reviewing)
- Lifestyle inflation (spending more as income increases)

### Step 4: Calculate Key Metrics
- Savings rate: (Income - Expenses) / Income × 100
- Needs vs. Wants ratio: Essential expenses / Total expenses
- Dining out ratio: Restaurant spending / Total food spending
- Financial freedom number: Annual expenses × 25

## Red Flags in Spending
- Housing exceeds 35% of income
- Total debt payments exceed 20% of income (excluding mortgage)
- No money left for savings after essential expenses
- Relying on credit cards for basic needs
- Spending more than earned for 2+ consecutive months
- Subscription total exceeds 10% of income

## Seasonal Spending Patterns
- January: New Year resolutions (gym memberships, courses)
- March-April: Tax preparation expenses
- May-June: Wedding season, travel bookings
- August-September: Back-to-school, new routines
- November-December: Holiday spending spike (plan for 1.5x normal)

## Cash Flow Optimization
- Align bill due dates with pay dates
- Build a 1-month buffer (one month of expenses in checking)
- Use credit cards for rewards, pay in full monthly
- Identify money "leaks" - small charges that accumulate
