# Savings Tips and Strategies

## Emergency Fund
An emergency fund should cover 3-6 months of essential expenses. Keep it in a high-yield savings account for easy access. Start with a $1,000 starter fund, then build to the full amount.

Priority order:
1. $1,000 starter emergency fund
2. Pay off high-interest debt (above 7%)
3. Build full 3-6 month emergency fund
4. Invest for long-term goals

## High-Yield Savings Accounts
Traditional savings accounts offer 0.01-0.05% APY. High-yield savings accounts (HYSAs) offer 4-5% APY. For a $10,000 emergency fund, that's $400-500/year in interest vs. $1-5.

## Reducing Expenses - Quick Wins
- Audit subscriptions monthly: Cancel unused services (average person wastes $30-50/month)
- Negotiate bills annually: Internet, insurance, phone plans (average savings: $50-100/month)
- Cook at home: Average meal out costs $15-25 vs. $4-7 at home
- Use the 24-hour rule: Wait 24 hours before non-essential purchases over $50
- Automate savings: Set up automatic transfers on payday

## The Latte Factor
Small daily expenses compound significantly:
- $5/day coffee = $1,825/year
- $12/day lunch out = $4,380/year
- $15/day combined small expenses = $5,475/year

Not about never enjoying coffee - about being intentional about which small expenses bring real joy.

## Savings Rate Benchmarks
- Minimum: 10% of gross income
- Good: 15-20% of gross income
- Excellent: 25-35% (early retirement track)
- FIRE movement: 50%+ (financial independence in 10-15 years)

## Sinking Funds
Set aside money monthly for predictable irregular expenses:
- Car maintenance: $100/month
- Holiday gifts: $50-100/month
- Annual insurance premiums: divide by 12
- Vacation fund: $100-200/month
- Home repairs: 1% of home value annually, divided by 12

## Debt Payoff Strategies
### Avalanche Method
Pay minimum on all debts, put extra money toward highest interest rate first. Mathematically optimal - saves the most money in interest.

### Snowball Method
Pay minimum on all debts, put extra money toward smallest balance first. Psychologically motivating - quick wins build momentum.

## Investing Basics
- Start with employer 401(k) match (free money)
- Then max Roth IRA ($7,000/year in 2024)
- Index funds offer diversification at low cost (0.03-0.10% expense ratio)
- Time in market beats timing the market
- Compound interest: $500/month at 10% for 30 years = $1.1 million
